Bio (CV, Google scholar)
I am a principal scientist at Wayve and an associate professor of Computing Science at Simon Fraser University. Prior to SFU, I was an assistant professor at Washington University in St. Louis. Before WUSTL, I was a software engineer at Google. Before Google, I was a post-doctoral research associate at University of Washington. I worked with Prof. Seitz and Prof. Curless at University of Washington, and Rick Szeliski at Facebook (was at Microsoft Research). I completed my Ph.D. under the supervision of Prof. Ponce at Computer Science Department of University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in May 2008. I received PAMI Longuet-Higgins prize (2020), CS-CAN: Outstanding Young CS Researcher Award (2018), NSF CAREER Award (2015), Best Student Paper Award at ECCV 2012, and Google Faculty Research Awards (2018, 2017, 2016).

How to join my group?

I hold a joint appointment with Wayve and SFU. As a result, I keep my SFU research group intentionally small and recruit only a limited number of students. Our research focuses on embodied AI, including autonomous driving, rather than traditional computer vision or 3D computer vision. If you are interested in joining my group, please email me your CV or resume. Due to the high volume of inquiries, I won't be able to respond individually.
